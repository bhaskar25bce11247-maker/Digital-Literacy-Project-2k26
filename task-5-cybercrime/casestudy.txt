Case Study – Cyberbullying: 


1. What is Cyberbullying? 
  
Cyberbullying is a type of online harassment where individuals use digital platforms like social 
media, messaging apps, or gaming communities to harm others. According to UNICEF, more 
than 1 in 3 young people in over 30 countries report experiencing online bullying, showing how 
widespread the issue is. 
  
2. How It Happens

Cyberbullying usually begins with a small negative interaction, such as a rude comment or joke. 
It then escalates into repeated actions like sending abusive messages, spreading false rumors, 
or sharing private images without consent. In many cases, bullies create fake accounts to hide 
their identity. Over time, others may join in, amplifying the harassment and making it harder for 
the victim to escape. 
  
3. Who is Targeted?
  
Teenagers and college students are the most common targets, especially those who are shy, 
new to a group, or perceived as “diAerent.” Studies by Pew Research Center show that 59% of 
teens in the U.S. have experienced some form of online harassment.

4. Consequences 
  
The eAects of cyberbullying include anxiety, depression, low self-esteem, and poor academic 
performance. In severe cases, it can lead to self-harm or suicidal thoughts. Since online content 
spreads quickly and remains accessible, the impact is often long-lasting and diAicult to control. 
  
5. Prevention 
  
Cyberbullying can be reduced through awareness, reporting abusive behavior, using privacy 
settings, and promoting respectful online communication.
